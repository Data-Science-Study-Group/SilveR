Analysis description

The data were analysed using a 3-way repeated measures mixed model approach, with Treat 1 and Treat 2 as treatment factors, Day 1 as the repeated factor and Cov1, Cov6 and Cov7 as covariates.This was followed by Planned Comparisons on the predicted means to compare the levels of the Selected effect. The response was Log10 transformed prior to analysis to stabilise the variance. The covariate was also Log10 transformed.

The unstructured covariance structure allowed the variability of the responses to be different, depending on the level of Day 1. This structure also allowed the correlation between responses from any pair of levels of Day 1 to be different. While this approach is the most general it should be used with care when there are few subjects, as many parameters are required to be estimated. These estimates may not be very reliable.

A full description of mixed model theory, including information on the R nlme package used by InVivoStat (beta version), can be found in Venables and Ripley (2003) and Pinherio and Bates (2002).

For more information on the theoretical approaches that are implemented within this module, see Bate and Clark (2014).
